## Reading This Drawing

**The Two Boxes:** On the left is YOU. On the right is AI. These are two different minds with different strengths. Neither can build software alone—together they can build anything.

**Your Side (Left Box):** You bring four things:
- **Problem:** What you're trying to solve. "I need to calculate beam deflection" or "I need to organize these files."
- **Context:** Background the AI can't see. Your constraints, preferences, what you've already tried.
- **Intent:** What outcome you want. Not how to code it—what it should do.
- **Judgment:** Only you can decide if the result is right. AI can't know if the output makes sense for your situation.

**AI's Side (Right Box):** AI brings four things:
- **Code:** Every programming language, every syntax rule.
- **Patterns:** Standard ways to solve common problems. Data processing, web apps, calculations.
- **Syntax:** The exact characters and structure code needs.
- **Libraries:** Thousands of pre-built tools for any task.

**The Bridge (Center):** The PROMPT connects both sides. Your words become AI's instructions. The quality of this bridge determines the quality of what you build.

**The Output (Bottom):** SOFTWARE emerges from the collaboration. Not from you alone. Not from AI alone. From both sides contributing what they do best.

## Key Insight

You never need to learn the right side. AI has it. Your job is only the left side: know your problem, provide context, state intent, apply judgment.

## What This Means In Practice

When you sit down to build something:
1. You don't think "how do I code this?"
2. You think "what do I need?" and "how do I describe it clearly?"

The engineering knowledge you already have (formulas, requirements, constraints) is exactly what goes on the left side. AI handles the right side automatically.
